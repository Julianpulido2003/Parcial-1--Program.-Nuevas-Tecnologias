Why do I find augmented reality and virtual reality interesting?

Augmented reality and virtual reality are two cutting-edge technologies that were only born in the last century. Augmented reality came about thanks to the scientist and researcher Thomas P. Caudell who, while developing one of the most famous planes in the world: the Boeing 747, He observed that the operators in charge of assembling the new plane were wasting too much time interpreting the instructions, and he thought: What if they had access to a screen to guide them through the installation? The invention did not prosper, but at that precise moment the concept of Augmented Reality was born.

While in virtual reality it takes a longer evolution process but until 1987 it bears the name given to it by Jaron Lanier and Tom Zimmerman, who would be one of the main actors who developed the "data glove", which was a big step. . . . . . . . in the field of haptic virtual reality.

Impact on the world

These technologies have revolutionized the world in an impressive way and are used for many things, for example: to carry out detailed surgeries and to be able to practice before the operation, to make engineering designs with the possibility of being able to see them physically, also to carry out facial scans and being able to recognize people's faces, create new video games with new experiences and revolutionize this industry, among others.

Difference between augmented reality and virtual reality

It is very interesting how these have revolutionized many sciences and have helped the world to grow. However, there is a clear difference between these two technologies, despite being intertwined, they pose different revolutions. While Virtual Reality allows us to create a virtual world from scratch with everything we want, but a fantastic world, what Augmented Reality does is add virtual elements (additional information in the form of graphics or images) to our real environment.

In addition, important people such as the CEO of Apple have said: "Augmented Reality (AR) encompasses more than Virtual Reality (VR) because it gives us the possibility of being present and communicating and, at the same time, enjoying other things at a visual". level. It will be the next revolution, as the smartphone was at that time."
